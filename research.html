Put broadly, I am interested in knowing what information can we extract about complex, networked, dynamical systems simply by observing their behavior over time. For example, if we can see how the neurons inside an animal's brain are firing, is it possible to know how they are wired together? If we observe the eruptions of Yellowstone geysers over a long period of time, what information do that reveal about how the geysers are influencing each other underground? Like these examples, we often cannot literally dig deep into such complex systems - it is hard to dissect small, delicate brains of lab animals and observe neuronal wiring there in microspic details, it is too risky or impossible to go underground where the Yellowstone geysers are transporting material into each other, and so on. Rather, we can only stand by and see how the behavior of these systems change over time. So, a common question is what information do these dynamical behaviors reveal to us about what is going on inside these complex systems? This is why I am developing new computer codes based on artificial intelligence and machine learning that tell us how the inner workings of complex systems can be backtraced from their dynamics.
